Is Your Food Worth It's Weight In Exercise?

Breakfast Cereals

While many have a reputation as a health food, it pays to read the labels. Breakfast cereals can have as much sugar as candy bars, and research shows that 92% of people pour far more than one serving into their bowls.

Coffee

What’s the big deal? One cup of coffee or espresso is the same as any other, right? Wrong. A cup of brewed coffee with a splash of skim milk and a bit of sugar is less than 25 calories. A caramel macchiato, on the other hand, will set you back 300 calories.

(20 oz. caramel macchiato from starbucks is 300 calories = 2.2 miles)

Smoothies

We get it. The weather’s warming up and you want a refreshing snack or meal. However, many popular brands of smoothie can pack in over 450 calories. Again, you need to check the label – and the serving size – to make sure you’re not sucking down a calorie monster.

(28 oz. Strawberries Wild Jamba Juice Smoothie is 470 calories = 3.5 miles)

Fast Food Salads

While it’s great to see fast food outlets attempting healthy alternatives, many fast food salads are burdened with fat-laden dressing, sodium-rich croutons, and other problematic toppings. Instead, make salad at home in the morning, so you can just grab it and go in the afternoon.

(Bacon Ranch Crispy Chicken w/ dressing from McDonald’s is 570 calories = 4.2 miles)

Frozen Meals

Even foods that are labeled as “healthy” or “light” can contain high levels of sodium, sugar, and preservatives. When you have some free time, make your own meals at home and freeze them. Then it will be easy to thaw and serve on those busy evenings.

(Frozen Personal Pepperoni Pizza from DiGiorno’s is 770 calories = 5.7 miles)

Sports Drinks

Going to grab a sports drink to quench your thirst and replenish your electrolytes after a workout? You may replace all those calories you just burned! There’s very little evidence that sports drinks have a positive effect on your health. Have a glass of water or chocolate milk instead!
